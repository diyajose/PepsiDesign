Pepsi is a world-renowned carbonated soft drink, known for its bold, refreshing taste and its iconic blue, red, and white branding. It was created in 1893 by Caleb Bradham, a pharmacist from New Bern, North Carolina, and was originally named "Brad's Drink". It was renamed as Pepsi-Cola in 1898, and then shortened to Pepsi in 1961.
